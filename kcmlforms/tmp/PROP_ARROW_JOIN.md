*(None, Start, End, Both)*

**Specifies which ends of the line will join with another line**

The join property is used to draw arrows with flat ends that can be butted up to other arrows in order to make more complicated arrow shapes. The joins can be at the start end or at both ends of the arrow.

In this example the arrows do not have a join style. There is a visible joint between them.

<img src="/bitmaps/arrowjoin1.png" data-align="BOTTOM" data-border="0" width="250" height="200" alt="no join" />

With joins set on the two ends that meet, the joint is seamless.

<img src="/bitmaps/arrowjoin2.png" data-align="BOTTOM" data-border="0" width="250" height="200" alt="seamless join" />
